About Charlotte Chamber of Commerce

Charlotte Chamber of Commerce is a leading membership-driven local chamber located in Charlotte, North Carolina. It has members all across Charlotte Metro, Charlotte City. It was founded in 1876 and is a registered member of the U.S. Chamber of Commerce.

Charlotte Chamber of Commerce’s mission statement broadly covers and conveys - The organisation is to provide members opportunities for growth, collaboration and education.

Working as a Charlotte’s small business community, the Chamber has created strategic partnerships within the local business network, which has resulted in a mutually beneficial relationship.

Charlotte Chamber of Commerce is committed to focus on Economic Development, Advanced Manufacturing, Healthcare, Information Technology, Logistics and Distribution. It leverages the expertise of local government, city council members and community representatives to develop pro-business initiatives. For strengthening, referring and connecting local businesses, the organization also works with Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs).

The organization helps small businesses in members-only discounts and services, business owners can attend ongoing training and education sessions, business advocacy for your business, getting name in online membership directory, access to exclusive resources, valuable partnership opportunities through business - event tie ups, SEO benefits with website citation, exclusive mailing list access, portraying yourself as a local expert in your business type, marketing and sponsorship opportunities through digital as well as physical mailers, added promotion through chambers social media, relocation and expansion support.

The Chamber has many benefits, services and discounts that it offers to its membership holders. Some of the benefits include small business loans and commercial finance, building a successful social entrepreneurship, relocation aid, supporting women entrepreneurship, budgeting and financial forecasting, improving operational efficiency, improving marketing strategies, recruitment and finding right candidate, understanding international trade commission, getting affordable small business insurance, finding federal government contracting opportunities, managing business finances, buying and leasing business equipment’s and many more.

The trade union has a wide variety of events from monthly networking mixers to annual luncheons to build and enhance member businesses. It offers engaging events such as Economic Outlook Conference, Business Showcase, Ballantyne Business Bash, Ballantyne Chapter Luncheon, Annual Business Bash.

Charlotte Chamber of Commerce has partnered with Novant Health, Bank of America, CBI Interior, Premier Inc., Belk, Inc. for the economic development and growth of greater Charlotte, North Carolina.
