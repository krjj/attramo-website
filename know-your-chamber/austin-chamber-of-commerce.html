About Austin Chamber of Commerce

Austin Chamber of Commerce is a leading membership-driven local chamber located in Austin, Texas. It has members all across Austin city, Round Rock, Cedar Park, Georgetown, San Marcos. It was founded in 1932 and is a registered member of the U.S. Chamber of Commerce.

Austin Chamber of Commerce’s mission statement broadly covers and conveys - Provide leadership that facilitates the creation of a prosperous regional economy and effective advocacy for its Partners.

Working as a Austin’s small business community, the Chamber has created strategic partnerships within the local business network, which has resulted in a mutually beneficial relationship.

Austin Chamber of Commerce is committed to focus on Economic Development, Taxes & Incentives, Federal/State Advocacy and Education/Talent Development. It leverages the expertise of local government, city council members and community representatives to develop pro-business initiatives. For strengthening, referring and connecting local businesses, the organization also works with Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs).

The organization helps small businesses in getting more customer referrals, providing legislative services, directing access to chamber events and programs, free publicity with local business promotional guides, members-only discounts and services, business owners can attend ongoing training & education sessions, business advocacy for your business, exclusive mailing list access, portraying yourself as a local expert in your business type, marketing and sponsorship opportunities through digital as well as physical mailers, added promotion through chambers social media, relocation and expansion support, providing market information, engaging with like-minded peers, member directory listing, providing group health, opportunity to be listed in the chamber of commerce visitor guide.

The Chamber has many benefits, services and discounts that it offers to its membership holders. Some of the benefits include applying and getting permit and vendor license, developing HR policies and human resource management, small business administration, creating a business plan and building entrepreneurial skills, setting up business expansion strategies, small business loans and commercial finance, building a successful social entrepreneurship, relocation aid, supporting women entrepreneurship, budgeting and financial forecasting, improving operational efficiency, improving marketing strategies, recruitment and finding right candidate, understanding international trade commission and many more.

The trade union has a wide variety of events from monthly networking mixers to annual luncheons to build and enhance member businesses. It offers engaging events such as Annual Meeting, Austin Gives Generous Business Awards, Economic Outlook Summit, PowerUp Austin! Series, Austin A-List of the Hottest Startups.

Austin Chamber of Commerce has partnered with Seton, Wells Fargo, Bank of America, Dell EMC, Brookfield Residential for the economic development and growth of greater Austin, Texas.
