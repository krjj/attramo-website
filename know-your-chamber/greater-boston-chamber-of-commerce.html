About Greater Boston Chamber of Commerce

Greater Boston Chamber of Commerce is a leading membership-driven local chamber located in Boston, Massachusetts. It has members all across Boston, Cambridge, Newton. It is a registered member of U.S. Chamber of Commerce.

Greater Boston Chamber of Commerce’s mission statement broadly covers and conveys - We are the convener, voice and advocate of our diverse and collaborative membership who drive Greater Boston's success.

Working as a Boston’s small business community, the Chamber has created strategic partnerships within the local business network, which has resulted in a mutually beneficial relationship.

Greater Boston Chamber of Commerce is committed to focus on Economic Development, Business Advocacy. It leverages the expertise of local government, city council members and community representatives to develop pro-business initiatives. For strengthening, referring and connecting local businesses, the organization also works with Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs).

The organization helps small businesses in improving your business visibility, developing business network, influencing government by becoming a voice of business, free chamber newsletter with offers and other local businesses information, providing legislative services, directing access to chamber events and programs, free publicity with local business promotional guides, business owners can attend ongoing training and education sessions, business advocacy for your business, SEO benefits with website citation, exclusive mailing list access, portraying yourself as a local expert in your business type, marketing and sponsorship opportunities through digital as well as physical mailers, added promotion through chambers social media, opportunity to be listed in the chamber of commerce visitor guide, promoting broader pro-business initiatives, new member mentoring program.

The Chamber has many benefits, services and discounts that it offers to its membership holders. Some of the benefits include small business administration, creating a business plan and building entrepreneurial skills, managing business finances, buying and leasing business equipment’s, business relocating or expanding to new locations help, import and export assistance, technical assistance training, setting up business expansion strategies, small business loans and commercial finance, improving marketing strategies, recruitment and finding right candidate, understanding international trade commission, getting affordable small business insurance, finding federal government contracting opportunities and many more.

The trade union has a wide variety of events from monthly networking mixers to annual luncheons to build and enhance member businesses. It offers engaging events such as Government Affairs Forums, Women's Network, City Awake, Chamber Networking Group, Pinnacle Awards, Small Business of the Year Awards, Ten Outstanding Young Leaders Awards.

Greater Boston Chamber of Commerce has partnered with Bank of America, Blue Cross Blue Shield of Massachusetts, Citizens Bank, John Hancock, KPMG for the economic development and growth of greater Boston, Massachusetts.
